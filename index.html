Light Attacks 101

What You Need To Know

What is a light attack? I was hoping you would ask. A light attack is an insult or jab that involves no fowl or inappropriate language but instead focuses on inconviences everyone experiences. These types of remarks are extremely effective as they are often unexpected and cause no permanent damage to the relationship. These are best used against friends, parents, or perhaps to difuse a tense situation. Whatever the reason, light attacks are an easy way to get under someone's skin with out offending them. Without further ado, let's get into it.



"I hope the line is super long when you go to Walmart."

A simple yet frustrating inconvenience can be enough to send someone over the edge.



"I hope your eyes are half closed in every photo."

From family photos to LinkedIn profile pictures, not looking your best is a pet peeve that ticks off anyone and everyone.



"I hope your fast food order gets messed up."

Any person will tell you, when their simple burger fries with a frosty order isn't what's in the bag, a rage unlike any other ensues.



"I hope you bite into a chip vertically."

...it's a pain unlike anyother...



"I hope there's a spider in your bed tonight."

The mere suggestion of this is enough give anyone the heebie jeebies and a night of insomnia.



"I hope your parlay misses by one game."

So close to greatness only to lose it all.



"I hope you get blamed for someone else's fart."

Nothing is worse than public embarrasement - especially when you're falsely accused.



"I hope you stutter horribly during your next presentation."

Ah yes - more public humiliation... And EVERYONE is watching.



"I hope your acne flares up before a formal event."

It's simply Murphy's law and by gosh it's embarrasing and frustrating.



"I hope your TV remote is out of battery."

Straight pain. All comfy in the couch and this happens. Pain.



"I hope there's bird poop on your car"

Specifically right after you've washed it.



"I hope the soap doesn't get off the sunscreen in the shower."

It makes me feel icky - like I'm covered in slime.
